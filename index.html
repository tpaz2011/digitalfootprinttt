DIGITAL FOOTPRINT

WHAT IS DIGITAL FOOTPRINT?

A digital footprint (sometimes called a digital shadow or electronic footprint) refers to the trail of data you leave behind when you use the Internet. This includes the websites you visit and the emails and information you send online and if you don´t use with responsability it will be dangerous.

TIPS FOR TAKING CARE OF YOUR DIGITAL FOOTPRINT

Never share passwords with others. Passwords are personal and should be protected as much as you protect the keys to your house.
You should never click on sites that seem questionable. Only use information from reputable sites.
You never share that you are home alone. Posting that you are home without adult supervision may put you at risk for unwanted visitors.
It’s best to only share information with close friends and family members.

DRAWBACKS

A large digital footprint increases the risk of personal information being exposed or misused, leading to privacy breaches.
More data online means higher susceptibility to cyber attacks, identity theft, and other security threats.
Digital footprints can affect personal relationships and social interactions, as past online behavior and opinions may be scrutinized by others.
Constant awareness of being monitored and the pressure to maintain a positive online image can lead to stress and anxiety.

RESPONSIBILITIES AS A STUDENT

Use strong, unique passwords for your accounts and enable two-factor authentication whenever possible.
Avoid clicking on suspicious links or downloading files from unknown sources to protect yourself from malware and hacking.
Refrain from engaging in cyberbullying or harassing behavior towards others online.
Report any instances of bullying or harassment you encounter and support those who are affected by it.
Remember that potential employers, colleges, and scholarship committees may review your online presence.
Ensure that your digital footprint reflects your best self and highlights your skills, accomplishments, and interests positively.
Avoid sharing information about others without their consent. Respect their right to privacy and only post photos, videos, or personal information with their permission.

CONCLUSION

Your digital footprint shapes how others perceive you so it's important to curate a positive digital footprint that accurately reflects your values and achievements, have a a nice reputation and be respecful online too. A digital footprint is an indelible imprint of our online activities, comprising everything from social media posts and comments. This digital trail shapes how we are perceived by others, impacts our opportunities in education and employment, and raises important considerations. about privacy and security.
